Cricket Game Rules & Player Roles

🏏 Basic Cricket Rules

  • 11 players per team on the field at any time
  • Each team gets 50 overs (300 balls) to bat
  • 2 batters from the batting team are on the field at once
  • Batters score runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets
  • Boundaries: 4 runs (ball reaches boundary), 6 runs (ball clears boundary)
  • Bowlers deliver 6 balls per over
  • Teams switch between batting and fielding after 50 overs or 10 wickets

🎯 Player Roles Explained

  • Batters - Specialize in scoring runs, positioned in top-right of scatter plot
  • Bowlers - Specialize in taking wickets, positioned in bottom-left of scatter plot
  • All-rounders - Balanced players who both bat and bowl effectively
  • Batting All-rounders - Better at batting but contribute with bowling
  • Bowling All-rounders - Better at bowling but contribute with batting
  • Wicket-keeper Batters - Specialized fielders who also bat in top order

📊 Visualizer Interpretation Guide

  • X-axis represents Batting Impact (0-100 scale)
  • Y-axis represents Bowling Impact (0-100 scale)
  • Players in top-right are elite all-rounders
  • Players in bottom-right are pure batters
  • Players in top-left are pure bowlers
  • Spider chart shows Batting, Bowling & Fielding capabilities

🏆 Match Formats & Strategies

  • ODI Cricket - 50 overs per side, balanced approach needed
  • Team Balance - Ideal teams have 5-6 batters, 4-5 bowlers, 1-2 all-rounders
  • Powerplay - First 10 overs with fielding restrictions
  • Death Overs - Final 10 overs where aggressive batting occurs
  • Wicket Preservation - Key strategy in longer formats
  • Run Rate Management - Balancing aggression with wicket conservation

⭐ Key Player Metrics

  • Batting Average - Runs scored per dismissal
  • Strike Rate - Runs scored per 100 balls faced
  • Bowling Average - Runs conceded per wicket taken
  • Economy Rate - Runs conceded per over bowled
  • Fielding Efficiency - Catches taken and run-outs effected
  • Contribution Index - Overall impact on match outcome
