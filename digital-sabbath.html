The Rules of Your Digital Sabbath

A Digital Sabbath is a 24-hour period where you voluntarily disconnect from all digital devices: smartphones, computers, tablets, and TVs. The goal isn't to be unproductive, but to be differently productiveâ€”focusing on rest, creativity, and real-world connection.

  1. Choose Your 24 Hours: From Friday sunset to Saturday sunset, or any period that works for you.
  2. Power Down & Stash Away: Turn off all non-essential digital devices and put them in a drawer. Silence is golden.
  3. Embrace Analog: This planner will help you fill your time with non-digital activities. No "just checking."
  4. Tell Your Circle: Let friends and family know you'll be offline, so they don't worry.
  5. Be Kind to Yourself: If you slip up, don't despair. Acknowledge it and gently return to your analog day.
