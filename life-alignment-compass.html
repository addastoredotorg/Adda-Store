How to Use the Life Alignment Compass

  1. Assess each life area - Rate how aligned you feel in health, work, love, and purpose on a scale of 1-10
  2. View your compass - See a visual representation of your life balance and overall alignment
  3. Reflect on insights - Consider the personalized reflection questions for each area
  4. Identify patterns - Notice which areas need more attention and which are thriving
  5. Take action - Use the insights to make small, meaningful adjustments in your life

Understanding Alignment

Alignment means your daily life reflects your true values and priorities. High alignment indicates satisfaction and congruence between your actions and values. Low alignment suggests areas where changes might bring more fulfillment.

Interpreting Your Results

  • Balanced Compass (70%+): Your life areas are well-integrated and supporting each other
  • Moderate Alignment (50-69%): Some areas are thriving while others need attention
  • Low Alignment (Below 50%): Significant imbalance - focus on your lowest areas first
  • Extreme Differences: Large gaps between areas may indicate over-focus in some domains at the expense of others
