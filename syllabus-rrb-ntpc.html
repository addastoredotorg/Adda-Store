📊 Exam Pattern & Structure

1st Stage CBT

  • Duration: 90 Minutes (120 mins for PwBD)
  • Total Questions: 100
  • Total Marks: 100
  • Marking Scheme: +1 for correct answer
  • Negative Marking: ⅓ mark deducted for wrong answer

2nd Stage CBT

  • Duration: 90 Minutes (120 mins for PwBD)
  • Total Questions: 120
  • Total Marks: 120
  • Marking Scheme: +1 for correct answer
  • Negative Marking: ⅓ mark deducted for wrong answer
ℹ️
Note: The 1st Stage CBT is of screening nature. The standard of questions for the CBT will be generally in conformity with the educational standards prescribed for the posts.

🎯 Preparation Strategy & Tips

Time Management

Allocate time based on weightage - focus more on General Awareness (40-42%) followed by Mathematics and Reasoning equally.

Study Material

Focus on NCERT books (6th to 10th standard) for basic concepts. Use standard reference books for advanced practice.

Practice & Mock Tests

Take regular mock tests to improve speed and accuracy. Analyze your performance to identify weak areas.

Current Affairs

Read newspapers daily and follow monthly current affairs magazines. Focus on last 6-12 months of important events.

Reasoning Practice

Practice different types of reasoning questions daily. Focus on puzzles, syllogism, and coding-decoding.

Mathematics Focus

Concentrate on simplification, percentage, profit-loss, and time-speed-distance. Learn shortcut methods.

📌 Important Notes & Instructions

⚠️
Negative Marking: There will be negative marking of 1/3rd mark for every wrong answer in both stages of CBT.
ℹ️
Medium of Examination: Questions will be available in English, Hindi, and 13 regional Indian languages (Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Konkani, Malayalam, Manipuri, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu and Urdu).
📊
Normalization of Marks: Shortlisting of candidates for various stages shall be based on normalized marks obtained whenever CBT is conducted in multiple sessions.
